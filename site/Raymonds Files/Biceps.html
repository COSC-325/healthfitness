Bicep Workouts



Information About Biceps

The bicep is normally known to be the upper muscle of your arm passed your forearm. The technical term of a biceps is muscle having two points of attachment at one end. Now the purpose of the bicep (also known as the bicep brachii) is a "bi-articular muscle, which means it helps control the motion of two different joints, the shoulder and the elbow. The function of the biceps at the elbow is essential to the function of the forearm in lifting" (*source*).

Preacher Curls

Preacher curls can be used with dumbbells, stright barbell, or EZ barbell. The straight barbell is shown in the image above. To do this properly, you must fully extend your arm to get the most out of the exercise. Then bring your arms up to your chin and fully squeeze your biceps to really feel your biceps working.

Hammer Curls

Hammer curls are used with a dumbbell. To do this exercise properly, you have to start you hands down by your hips, then slowly bring your arms up with the top of the dumbbell faceing the ceiling. Make sure that your forearms do not go past being parallel to the ground. Once you reach the top make sure you squeeze your biceps to get the most out of this exercise.

Standing Dumbbell Curl

Slowly curl the biceps up as far as you can go and at the top make sure you squeeze your biceps then slowly bring bring them down. Then repeat.

Incline Dumbbell Curl

Sit down at a bench that has an incline to it. The starting position will be with both hands at the sides with a dumbbell in one of each hand. Slowly bring your arms toward your shoulders and really sqeeze your biceps as you move upward. Once reaching the top, slowly bring your arms back down to the starting position then repeat.

Cable Rope Curl

Attach a rope attachment to the pulley and face toward the machine. Grab hold of the rope and begin to move your biceps slowly up tp your chest. Once you reach the top bring the rope slowly back down to the staring position and repeat.

Cable Flex Curl

Adjust the machine so the cable is level with your shoulders. Then grab the left side handle then the right side handle. Slowly bring your hands toward your head. Once you can't go any futher, squeeze your biceps then slowly bring them back the starting position. Then repeat.
